# Humane technology, in service of people — not capital.

---

Barrett Bryan-Soron · 2026-05-11

---

<!-- _class: prose -->

<p class="eyebrow">The argument</p>

## The default shape of software is extractive.

Most consumer software is built to convert attention into revenue. The interaction patterns follow: infinite scroll, push notifications, dark patterns at the unsubscribe link, friction where it serves the platform and ease where it serves the platform's growth.

This is not a bug. It's the rational consequence of *who pays for the software* and *what they're buying.*

So when we say **humane technology**, the work is to be specific about which incentive we are designing against.

---

<!-- _class: prose -->

<p class="eyebrow">A working definition</p>

## Humane technology is software whose business model is aligned with the user's outcome.

> Not "ethical software." Not "privacy-first." Not "user-centric." Aligned. The interests of the people running the company point in the same direction as the interests of the people using the thing.

---

<!-- _class: compare -->

<p class="eyebrow">Two business models, two products</p>

## Same nominal feature. Different incentive. Different software.

<div class="cols">

<div class="col">

<p class="col-label">Ad-supported</p>

### Social feed

- Optimises time-on-app — the metric the advertiser pays for.
- Algorithmic surfacing favours emotional volatility.
- Chronological view is hidden or removed.
- Export is missing or deliberately incomplete.

</div>

<div class="col">

<p class="col-label">Paid, user-owned</p>

### RSS reader

- Optimises read-then-leave — the user's actual goal.
- Surfacing is chronological by default; rules are user-defined.
- "Mark all read" is a first-class action.
- OPML import and export are core, not afterthoughts.

</div>

</div>

<p class="eyebrow">Tells</p>

## What to look for when you can't see the contract.

| Tell | Aligned | Extractive |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Cancellation | One click, in the same place as sign-up. | Buried, phone-only, or "talk to a specialist". |
| Export | All your data, in an open format, today. | A subset, in a proprietary format, on request. |
| Notifications | Off by default, granular controls. | On by default, bundled toggles. |
| Pricing | Public, flat, paid by the user. | Hidden, tiered, paid by a third party. |

---

<!-- _class: prose -->

<p class="eyebrow">In closing</p>

## Designers don't choose the business model. But we choose the defaults.

Most of the difference between an aligned product and an extractive one shows up in defaults — what's on, what's off, what's one click and what's three. We don't always get to pick the contract. We almost always get to pick the defaults.

**Pick like someone will live inside the result.**
