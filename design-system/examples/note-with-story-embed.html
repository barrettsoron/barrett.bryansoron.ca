I made a vertical re-cut of the Fastmail/Proton note this morning. Six cards. The idea is that the long essay is the one I want to be honest in, and the cards are the one I want to be portable in — passable, sharable, swipeable without losing the argument.

It is below. The arrow keys work, or you can click the link to open it in its own page.

What I find when I do this — take the same argument and cast it into a different medium — is that the parts that survive the recasting are the parts the argument actually needs. The ones that don't survive were doing rhetorical work, not logical work. That's useful to find out.

I think there's a version of this exercise that's just edit your essay by writing the deck of it. If a paragraph can't be a card, the paragraph is probably hedging.
