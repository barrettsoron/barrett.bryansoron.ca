[Matthias Ott](https://matthiasott.com) — German web designer, thoughtful writer — runs a personal site with a structural choice I've thought about for years: a clean **Notes / Articles** split.

Notes are short, frequent, low-stakes. A paragraph about a tool, a thing overheard, a link with a sentence. Articles are longer, slower, considered — the kind of thing that earns its own feed entry.

The useful thing about the split isn't the publishing cadence. It's the *permission structure*. Without a notes channel, every post you publish carries the weight of an article. That friction costs posts. It also bends your writing toward a kind of performed seriousness that isn't actually where the best thinking happens.

A notes stream relieves the pressure. It becomes the place where half-formed ideas get written down in public, where other people's work gets pointed at, where the day's small observations live. And, paradoxically, the articles get better — because they stop being the only thing.

I've built the same split here. The thing you're reading is a note. The essays sit one tab over.
