A running log of what I've actually closed the cover on this year. Not a list of what I'm partway through or intend to get to.

- ***Gathering Moss*** — Robin Wall Kimmerer. Read slowly, one essay a week. The best kind of science writing: patient, precise, grateful.
- ***The Overstory*** — Richard Powers. Took two tries. Worth it.
- ***How to Do Nothing*** — Jenny Odell. Reread. Lands differently at thirty-seven than it did at thirty-two.
- ***The Dawn of Everything*** — Graeber & Wengrow. Dense. Still chewing.

> The question is not what we can do with our attention but what we want our attention to be able to do. — Odell

Next on the nightstand: Zadie Smith's *Intimations*. Short, which is the right length for April.

A quiet boast: zero books abandoned this year. Either I'm getting better at choosing or I'm getting worse at quitting.
